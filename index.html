Here is some basic boilerplate html and a mini example of css structure. If we take a look at the structure of html, it's pretty comparable to word or google docs. Reading from top to bottom, we see the doc type declaration and language (html) declaration. Beneath that we have our header, not unlike the header you'd find in word. Within that is the title of the website, this is what appears in your broswers tab.

A quick note that these are link or anchor <a> buttons crafted using css, and not form buttons <button>. Buttons are important for guiding your users to properly use your links. Skeuomorphic refers to the design and how it looks almost like a button in real life. These are typically best because they are universally recognized as usable buttons. Flat buttons take on the concept that people have been using the web or your site long enough and understand the flow of how things work.
